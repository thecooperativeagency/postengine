## Hiring Strategy

### Phase 1 — Freelance First (Now)
- Start with freelancers for design + photography/video
- Lower risk, test quality before committing
- Use platforms: Upwork, Contra, LinkedIn, local creative networks
- Referrals from existing network preferred

### Phase 2 — Part-Time Conversion (Q3 2026)
- Convert best freelancers to part-time retainer
- Social media manager as first hire (highest recurring need)
- Graphic designer second

### Phase 3 — Full-Time Staff (2027+)
- When client base hits 8-10 rooftops
- In-house creative team makes economic sense

## Management Ideas

### Structure
- Lance = Creative Director + Account Lead (stays close to clients)
- Each role reports directly to Lance for now
- As team grows: Creative Lead to manage design/photo/video team

### Tools
- **Project management:** Notion or Monday.com (assign briefs, track deliverables)
- **File sharing:** Google Drive (already set up)
- **Communication:** Slack or Teams (keep client work organized by channel)
- **Scheduling:** Buffer or Later for social media scheduling

### Standards to Set From Day One
- Brand voice guide per client (tone, words to use/avoid)
- No stock images — ever
- All content goes through Lance approval before posting
- Weekly check-in (15 min) per team member
- Monthly performance review

### Compensation Philosophy
- Pay well for quality — cheap creative = cheap brand
- Freelancers get paid on delivery, net 7 days
- Full-time gets benefits + performance bonus tied to client retention
