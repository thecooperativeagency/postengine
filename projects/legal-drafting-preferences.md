# The Cooperative — Legal Document Drafting Preferences

## Overview
This document defines the standard legal, structural, and stylistic preferences for contracts created by The Cooperative (Marketing Agency). These preferences are derived from existing agreements with automotive clients and should be treated as the default framework unless otherwise specified.

## 1. Core Contract Structure

All contracts should follow this standardized section order:

1. Parties & Effective Date 
2. Scope of Services 
3. Payment Terms 
4. (Optional) Co-Op / Reimbursement Clause 
5. Contract Duration 
6. Confidentiality & Non-Disclosure 
7. Competition / Non-Compete (if applicable) 
8. Termination Clause 
9. Intellectual Property 
10. Insurance 
11. (Optional) Office Hours / Availability / Trade Agreements 
12. Negotiation / Amendment Clause 
13. Governing Law 
14. Signatures 

---

## 2. Scope of Services (Standardized Offering)

Unless customized, always include:

### SEO
- Blog posts, specials, incentives
- Locally relevant photography
- Original content

### SEM
- Campaign creation, management, optimization
- Ongoing updates to specials/incentives

### Social Media
- Custom photo/video production
- Viral reels + educational content
- Rapid updates for promotions

### Creative & Consulting
- Photography, videography, graphic design
- Campaign development
- CRM + lead platform management (Autotrader, KBB, Cargurus, etc.)

### Reporting
- Regular performance reporting
- Optimization insights

Variation Rule: 
If client prefers vendors (e.g., Jackson contract), shift to:
- "Assist in selecting and managing partners"
- "Apply best practices oversight"

---

## 3. Payment Terms (Standard)

Default:
- Monthly fee ($5,000–$6,000 depending on client)
- Due on the 1st of each month
- Net 15 payment terms after invoice

Optional additions:
- Co-op reimbursement language (BMW-specific)
- Trade/credit offsets (office lease agreements)

---

## 4. Contract Duration

Always:
- "In perpetuity"
- 30-day termination notice by either party

This is a non-negotiable structural preference unless explicitly changed.

---

## 5. Confidentiality Standards

Always include:
- Mutual or one-way confidentiality (depending on deal)
- Protection during AND after contract (perpetuity)
- Restrictions on meeting competitors on client property
- Restrictions on meeting outside clients at client locations

---

## 6. Competition & Exclusivity Preferences

When included, structure in tiers:

### Initial Freeze
- No new automotive clients for 3 months

### Extended Restriction
- No automotive clients for 6 months total

### Brand Protection
- No competing luxury brands (Audi, BMW, Lexus, Mercedes-Benz) in Louisiana for 1 year

### Geographic Lockouts (High-Value Clients)
- No Audi/BMW work in Baton Rouge, Lafayette, New Orleans (up to 10 years depending on contract)

Rule: 
Only include strict non-competes for premium or flagship clients.

---

## 7. Termination Clause

Always:
- 30 days written notice
- Payment required for work completed up to termination date

Keep language simple and enforceable.

---

## 8. Intellectual Property

Standard position:
- The Cooperative retains ownership until full payment
- Client receives non-exclusive usage rights after payment

Never default to full ownership transfer unless explicitly negotiated.

---

## 9. Insurance Requirements

Always include:
- Drive-out form + insurance required for anyone operating client vehicles
- The Cooperative maintains liability coverage

---

## 10. Office Hours / Onsite Expectations (Optional)

When applicable:
- ~25 hours/week onsite OR by appointment
- Remote availability: Mon–Sat, 7 AM – 9 PM
- Trade agreements may offset billing (e.g., rent exchange)

---

## 11. Negotiation / Amendment Clause

Always include:
- All terms are negotiable
- Changes must be:
  - Mutually agreed
  - Signed by both parties

---

## 12. Governing Law

Default:
- State where client operates

Examples:
- Louisiana (most contracts)
- Mississippi (Jackson contract)

---

## 13. Style & Tone Guidelines

- Plain English, minimal legal fluff
- Clearly structured sections with lettered subsections
- Avoid overly complex legal jargon
- Practical, business-first tone
- Contracts should feel firm but fair

---

## 14. Signature Block Format

Always include:
- Printed name
- Signature line
- Date line

Separate blocks for:
- The Cooperative
- Client

---

## 15. Customization Rules for OpenClaw

When generating contracts:

### Always Ask or Infer:
- Monthly fee
- State (governing law)
- Whether non-compete applies
- Whether co-op reimbursement applies
- Whether vendor oversight vs execution model is needed

### Default Assumptions:
- Perpetual contract
- 30-day termination
- Net 15 payment
- Non-exclusive IP license
- Full-service marketing scope

---

## 16. What NOT to Do

- Do not give away IP ownership by default 
- Do not omit termination clause 
- Do not create indefinite exclusivity without geographic limits 
- Do not overcomplicate legal language 
- Do not remove payment enforcement language 

---

## Final Note

These preferences represent The Cooperative's standard operating legal framework. Deviations should only occur when:
- Required by client negotiation
- Directed explicitly by leadership

Otherwise, this document should be treated as the default contract generation blueprint.
